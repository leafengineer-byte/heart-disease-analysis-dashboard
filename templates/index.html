About the project

Why analyze heart disease data?

Heart disease remains a leading cause of death worldwide. By studying demographic and lifestyle patterns in real-world data, this project helps identify signals that can guide earlier intervention, targeted education, and better resource allocation.

From raw survey data to insight

The underlying dataset captures self-reported health status, behavioral habits, and socio-demographic variables. Using Tableau dashboards and stories, we turn these rows of data into visual narratives about who is most at risk—and why.

Analysts, students, and clinicians can use this site as a starting point to ask better questions about prevention, early detection, and lifestyle interventions.

Demographics: age, sex, income Lifestyle: activity, smoking, BMI Clinical flags: stroke, diabetes Perceived health & mental health
Key questions we explore
  • How does heart disease prevalence shift across age and gender?
  • Which lifestyle factors appear most strongly associated with higher risk?
  • Are there patterns that suggest opportunities for targeted prevention?
