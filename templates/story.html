Key insights

What this story reveals

The exact numbers come from your Tableau visuals, but several recurring patterns typically emerge when combining demographic and lifestyle data for heart disease.

🎯
Risk rises sharply with age

Older age groups consistently show higher heart disease prevalence, especially when combined with additional risk factors such as diabetes or prior stroke.

🚬
Lifestyle compounding effects

Combinations of smoking, physical inactivity, and high BMI are associated with noticeably higher rates compared with individuals who maintain healthier habits.

🧩
Demographic disparities

Certain demographic slices—when controlled for age and behavior—still show elevated risk, hinting at structural or access‑related influences.

📌
Opportunities for prevention

The story points to cohorts where relatively small changes in behavior could translate into meaningful reductions in population‑level heart disease burden.
